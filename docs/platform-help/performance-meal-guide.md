# Performance Meal Guide

A day-by-day meal plan that turns each day's training-aware macro target into concrete planned meals, recipes, and "how much should I eat" portions — with planned-vs-eaten tracking.

## What it is (Athletes)

The Performance Meal Guide ("Måltidsguide för prestation" in Swedish) takes your training-aware daily targets and builds them into an actual day of eating: a set of timed meals (breakfast, snacks, lunch, dinner, pre/post-workout), each with its own calorie and macro target, a concrete recipe suggestion, and equivalent swap options. It classifies each day (rest, practice, hard practice, game, recovery, travel) and shapes the meals to that day — for example a game day front-loads easy carbohydrate and keeps the pre-game meal low in fat and fibre, while a rest day keeps protein high and carbohydrate calmer.

It is the "make it concrete" layer on top of the daily targets described in the Nutrition Features guide: the targets say *how much* of each macro; the meal guide says *which meals, when, what to cook, and how much of each food*.

## Where to find it

- **Dashboard → the Performance Meal Guide card** (and on the dedicated nutrition page for nutrition-focused athletes).
- Use **Generate / Uppdatera** on the card to create the guide or refresh it for the week.

## How it works

### Day classification and meal targets

Each day is classified from your schedule and gets a calorie + protein/carb/fat target (the same training-aware daily target used everywhere). The guide splits that target across the day's meal slots, so every planned meal shows its own kcal and P/C/F goal. An adaptation note explains the day (e.g. "weight is trending down, so the deficit is softened").

### Recipes with variety

Every planned meal comes with a concrete recipe — title, ingredients scaled to that meal's macro target, steps, and tips — plus a couple of equivalent swap options. Recipes are drawn from a rotating pool per meal type (breakfast, main meal, pre-fuel, recovery, snack), so the suggestions vary across days instead of repeating the same meal. Lunch and dinner default to different mains.

- **Surprise me / Överraska mig**: regenerate one meal's recipe to get a different idea.
- **Preference box**: type something like "I want chicken today" to steer that meal's recipe.
- The recipe matches the meal slot: a light snack gets a snack-sized recipe, a pre-workout slot gets easy-to-digest fuel, a recovery slot prioritises protein plus carbohydrate.

### "How much should I eat?" (portion calculator)

On each meal you can type the foods you actually want to eat (e.g. "yoghurt, banana") and get back **how many grams of each** to hit that meal's calorie/macro target, with the resulting totals. It resolves each food against the food database when it can and estimates with AI otherwise, then solves portions with calories as the priority. You can log the result as the eaten meal in one tap — which updates the planned-vs-eaten view.

### Planned vs eaten

The guide shows, per meal, what was planned versus what you've logged (kcal and each macro), so you can see at a glance which meals are still open and where you're ahead or behind for the day. Logging a meal — by photo, text, voice, the portion calculator, or the AI chat — updates it automatically when the meal matches a planned slot.

## Common questions

**Q: How is this different from the daily macro targets?**
A: The targets are the numbers; the meal guide turns them into specific meals, recipes, and gram amounts, and tracks what you actually ate against the plan.

**Q: I keep seeing the same recipes.**
A: Recipes rotate from a pool per day. Press **Uppdatera** to regenerate the guide, or **Surprise me** on a single meal for a different idea.

**Q: A snack suggested a full meal / the recipe seems too big.**
A: Recipes are scaled to each meal slot's macro target. If something looks off, regenerate it or use the portion calculator to size your own foods to the target.

**Q: Can I just tell it what I'm eating and have it size the portions?**
A: Yes — use the "How much should I eat?" box on the meal (or ask the floating AI chat), list your foods, and it returns grams per food to hit the target.

**Q: Does logging from the guide count toward my stats?**
A: Yes. Logged meals land in the normal meal log and feed adherence stats and the planned-vs-eaten view.
