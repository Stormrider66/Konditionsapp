# Getting Started: Your Athlete Dashboard

A guided tour of the athlete dashboard, the main navigation, and the daily habits that make the platform work for you.

## What it is (Athletes)

The athlete dashboard is your home screen. It shows today's training, your readiness, nutrition guidance, recent activity, and AI-powered cards — all in one place. Everything else (check-in, calendar, history, tests, settings) is one or two taps away in the top navigation.

## Where to find it

You land on **Dashboard** after logging in. The top navigation bar (or the menu button on mobile) always brings you back: **Dashboard → first item in the nav bar**.

## How it works

### Top navigation

The header has three top-level links plus two dropdown groups:

- **Dashboard** — your home screen.
- **Check-in** — the daily wellness check-in (takes under 2 minutes).
- **Calendar** — a unified calendar of your planned and completed sessions.
- **Training** (dropdown): History, My PRs, Programs, AI workouts (your WOD history), Training library, VBT data, Concept2, Video analysis, Team profile.
- **More** (dropdown): My profile, My coach, Tests & reports, Lactate test, Messages, Settings.

On mobile, all of these appear as one list in the menu. Your club's logo in the top-left corner always links back to the dashboard.

### What's on the dashboard

- **Welcome header with quick actions**: Log workout, Log food, Start session (jumps straight into today's first unfinished session) or Find session, Video analysis, and Report injury (when your club has a physio set up). An AI credit status strip shows your remaining AI allowance.
- **Hero card**: today's session(s). If you have several, you can swipe between them — program workouts come first, then coach assignments, then AI workouts. On days with nothing planned you see a rest-day card with your readiness and the next upcoming session.
- **Readiness panel**: your readiness score from the daily check-in, weekly training load vs. target, muscle fatigue, and any active injuries. If you haven't checked in today, it prompts you to do so.
- **Contextual cards** (appear when relevant): morning briefing, milestone celebrations, pre-workout nudge, pattern alerts, mental prep, nutrition timing, race fueling, post-workout check, and AI suggestions.
- **Sport-specific section**: extra widgets tailored to your primary sport (cycling, HYROX, team sports, racket sports, and more). You can switch the active sport if you have secondary sports.
- **Training and nutrition widgets**: upcoming workouts, weekly training summary, training trend chart, zone distribution, nutrition dashboard with today's macro targets, recent activity (including synced Strava/Garmin activities), active programs, AI workout summary, streak widget, and Garmin/Oura health cards if connected.

### Customizing the dashboard

Go to **More → Settings → Dashboard** to show, hide, and reorder widgets. A few safety-critical widgets (like active training restrictions) are always visible. Your coach can also set a default layout for the team; your own choices override it.

### The floating AI chat

A floating chat button is available on every athlete page (you can drag it to a different spot). It is your AI training assistant and can do real things, not just answer questions:

- Explain today's workout, your test results, or your training zones.
- Create a workout for today (an AI WOD) based on your readiness, injuries, and recent training.
- Log meals, edit or delete meals, and list what you ate recently.
- Log your daily check-in conversationally ("I slept badly and my legs are sore").
- Report pain or injury so your training can be adjusted.
- Update your profile details and add calendar events.
- Generate a complete multi-week training program (self-coached athletes with the right subscription; it builds in the background, typically 1–10 minutes).

Quick-prompt buttons get you started: "Explain today's workout", "How am I doing?", "Explain test", "Training zones", "Fuel for workout", "Motivation", "Pain/injury", "My progress". You can pick a model speed (fast / balanced / powerful), and the first time you use it you'll be asked for data-processing consent. Your subscription sets a monthly message allowance; a meter in the chat shows usage.

### The daily check-in habit

The single best habit on the platform: open **Check-in** every morning. It feeds your readiness score, which adjusts AI workouts, powers your morning briefing, and warns your coach early about fatigue or injury risk. It takes less than 2 minutes — see the "Daily check-ins and readiness" guide for details.

## Common questions

**Q: Where do I find today's workout?**
A: On the dashboard hero card, or via the "Start session" quick action. The full week is under Calendar, and the program view is under Training → Programs.

**Q: I don't see a widget I expect (e.g., the nutrition dashboard). Why?**
A: It may be hidden in your layout. Go to Settings → Dashboard and toggle it back on. Some widgets also only appear when relevant (e.g., race fueling only when you have a fueling plan).

**Q: What is the AI credit status strip?**
A: It shows how much of your AI allowance (chat messages, AI workouts, etc.) you have left on your current subscription tier.

**Q: Can the AI chat actually change things in my account?**
A: Yes — it can log check-ins, meals, and workouts, report injuries, and create calendar events. It always confirms what it did in the conversation.

**Q: How do I log a workout I did outside the platform?**
A: Use the "Log workout" quick action on the dashboard. You can write it, photograph a whiteboard, dictate it, import from Strava/Garmin, or fill in a form. See the "Workouts and logging" guide.

**Q: Where are my settings for language, theme, integrations, and subscription?**
A: More → Settings. It includes profile, password, subscription, appearance, training intensity, macro split, AI model preferences, notifications, privacy, integrations, and the dashboard widget layout.
