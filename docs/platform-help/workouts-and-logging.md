# Workouts and Logging

Every way athletes receive workouts on the platform, and every way to log what they actually did.

## What it is (Athletes)

Training can reach you through four channels: **program workouts** (multi-week training programs), **coach assignments** (individual strength, cardio, hybrid, and agility sessions), the **AI Workout of the Day (WOD)**, and **ad-hoc workouts** you log yourself. Everything you complete ends up in one combined training history and counts toward your training load.

## Where to find it

- Today's sessions: **Dashboard** hero card, or the "Start session" quick action.
- The week ahead: **Calendar**, and the "Upcoming workouts" dashboard widget.
- Programs: **Training → Programs**.
- AI workouts: **Training → AI workouts** (WOD history), or generate a new one from the dashboard.
- Self-logging: the **"Log workout"** quick action on the dashboard.
- Ready-made sessions: **Find workouts** (browse) and **Training → Training library**.
- Past training: **Training → History** and **Training → My PRs**.

## How it works

### Program workouts

A training program (built by your coach or generated by AI) is organized into weeks and days. Today's program workout appears on the dashboard hero card. Open it and choose how to run it:

- **Focus mode**: step through the workout exercise by exercise with set logging and a rest timer.
- **Quick log**: one form for the whole session — completion, duration, distance, per-segment details (pace, heart rate, power, reps), **perceived effort (RPE, 1–10)**, how it felt, notes, and optional fueling notes. Race-day workouts get a special flow where you enter your finish time and the platform assesses it against your goal.

Your coach can leave feedback on a logged workout; it appears at the top of the log page.

### Coach assignments (strength / cardio / hybrid / agility)

Coaches can assign standalone sessions outside a program: strength sessions, cardio sessions, hybrid (mixed-format) workouts, and agility workouts, each with an assigned date and optionally a training location. They show up on your dashboard for today and the coming week, and in the Training library with upcoming and completed lists. Each type has its own execution and logging flow; completing one marks the assignment as completed.

### AI Workout of the Day (WOD)

When you want a session that adapts to *today*, generate a WOD from the dashboard (for example from the Active programs card or the rest-day card). You pick, in steps:

1. **Workout type**: Strength, Cardio, Mixed (CrossFit/HYROX style), or Core.
2. **Mode**: Structured (science-based, adapted to your training plan), Casual, or Fun.
3. **Duration** (default 45 minutes).
4. **Equipment** — choose what you have; selecting a saved gym location auto-fills its equipment.

The generator reads your readiness score, recent check-in (fatigue, soreness, sleep), active injuries and physio restrictions, recent training, and weekly load, and caps the intensity accordingly (see the readiness guide). The workout opens in an execution view with warm-up / main / core / cool-down sections, exercise navigation, and a rest timer. On finishing, a completion screen asks for **session RPE (1–10)** and actual duration, and estimates the session's training load. **Free-tier athletes get 3 WODs per day; paid tiers are unlimited.** You can also ask the floating AI chat to "create a workout for today" — chat-created workouts land in the same place.

### Ad-hoc logging (training done outside the platform)

Quick action **"Log workout"** offers six input methods: **Write** (describe it in text), **Photo** (snap a whiteboard or paper), **Voice** (dictate it), **Strava import**, **Garmin Connect import**, and **Form** (structured manual entry). The AI parses your input into a structured workout, shows you a **review page**, and you confirm before it's saved. Confirmed ad-hoc workouts appear on the dashboard, in history, and in your training load.

### Browse workouts and the Training library

- **Find workouts** is a searchable catalog of ready-made workout templates with category filters (Strength, Cardio, Functional, Core, Stretching), a favorites filter, and a "your sport" filter.
- **Training library** collects your assigned material: upcoming and completed strength sessions, agility assignments and results, and your AI workout history.

### History, RPE, and PRs

**Training → History** merges everything: program workout logs, ad-hoc workouts, imported Garmin activities (deduplicated), coach assignments, and completed WODs — each labeled with its source (e.g., "AI workout", "AI chat", custom workout). Filter by timeframe (7 days, 30 days, and longer) and type; the page includes charts, totals (sessions, time, average effort), your personal records, and a data export button. RPE (perceived effort, 1–10) is collected on program logs and WOD completions and is used for effort averages and load estimates. **Training → My PRs** tracks personal records separately.

## Common questions

**Q: What does RPE mean and how do I pick a number?**
A: Rate of Perceived Exertion, 1–10. 1–3 feels very easy, 5–6 moderate, 7–8 hard, 9–10 maximal. It's your subjective feeling for the whole session and drives training-load estimates.

**Q: I did my run with Garmin/Strava — do I need to log it again?**
A: No. Use Log workout → Garmin/Strava import (or rely on the automatic sync if connected). History deduplicates imported activities against your logs.

**Q: Why don't I get a new WOD?**
A: On the free tier you get 3 per day — the dashboard shows how many remain. Paid tiers are unlimited. Also, with very low readiness or critical load warnings the WOD will be a recovery-style session by design.

**Q: Can I edit a logged workout?**
A: Yes — opening an already-logged program workout shows "edit" mode with your previous values prefilled. Ad-hoc workouts are editable on their review step before you confirm.

**Q: What's the difference between a program and an assignment?**
A: A program is a multi-week plan with a calendar of workouts. An assignment is a single session (strength/cardio/hybrid/agility) your coach schedules on a specific date, independent of any program.

**Q: Where do skipped sessions go?**
A: Assignments marked as skipped are removed from your dashboard's today/upcoming lists. Unlogged program workouts simply remain incomplete.
